Open questions / TBD

Only genuinely open questions live here; decided points are stated in the body text where they apply.

Wheel, tiers, and archetypes

  • Tiered mastery tuning: crafts-per-tier counts (the example uses about 20), the diminishing-returns curve below capability (zero at a two-tier delta), and which recipes each tier unlocks.
  • The hobby ceiling: rare, possibly epic per itemization; whether the hobby's cap is a hard cap; and the standalone hobby-switch quest cost (lower than an archetype switch, but high enough that flipping between the two opposites never becomes a de-facto second hobby).
  • Tier distribution against itemization: whether the specialist's working distribution (2 majors legendary, 1 hobby rare, 7 common) shifts to legendary / epic / uncommon, and the Jack of All Trades ceiling (uncommon vs rare across all ten) plus whether a Jack gets a higher combined ceiling to afford the breadth.
  • Jack of All Trades perks: the magnitude of the better/worse output variance and the material-saving bonus.

Attunement quests and legibility

  • The exact quest designs: the per-tier quest ladder (the first-time lore quest and each deeper tier quest) and the repeatable make-amends switch quest, and how its cost escalates with repeat switches (time, coin, materials, lore) while staying cheap enough early to let a player try a couple of archetypes.
  • Switching between an archetype and Jack of All Trades, in both directions: presumably the same attunement-quest pattern, but the mechanics, costs, and how the specialize direction's give-up-your-dabblings moment is staged are open.
  • The in-craft trend nudges (plain chat-log messages first, then lore-friendly delivery: guild mail, a courier), and the wording of the attunement quest's what-changes summary.

Items, binding, and commissions

  • The per-recipe bind-to-recipient policy: which output classes bind via boundTo, which tier threshold triggers it, and which required materials tie to the recipient.
  • The commission/order UX: order scope (open, guild, friends, or a specific crafter), cancel-before-accept, the crafter orders list, and the multi-step accept, craft, deliver flow.
  • Per-item-instance model: instance id + payload (signer, charges, rolled stats, the boundTo recipient), and how two rolled crafted items stack, list, and persist.

Output tuning

  • Output quality and ilvl budget: how the rarity-tier roll maps to stat rolls, and the exact curve over concentration, material tier, and skill.
  • Crafting-only effects: the design space and how they are balanced against item level.
  • Specialization perks: the exact perk set and the thresholds that unlock them.

Economy and sequencing

  • An output throttle for a maxed specialist, and a real gold sink in proportion to the new material faucet (the 5 percent market cut is the only recurring sink today).
  • Gathering-first sequencing: whether to accept material stockpiling before crafting ships, or add a soft mitigation.

Gathering, tools, and monster harvesting

  • Gathering focus: skill tree vs additive radar, focus granularity, and the re-spec cost curves.
  • Monster-tier to item-tier mapping: the shared rarity scale that the rarity-scaled effect consumption and tool gating read from.
  • Tool effects: the effect catalog, depletion rates, the rarity-scaled consumption curve, and recharge material costs.
  • Signed materials: the size of the self-gathered craft bonus.
  • Node density and dynamic / location-based respawn (explicitly future); persistence scope for respawn state across restarts.

Exploratory

  • Battlefield Experience: the observation radius (the 90-yard player scope, and whether group credit should reach farther than stranger credit), per-item attribution, the tier floor (uncommon or rare and above), and whether a Jack (who has no archetype majors) benefits at all, and if so for which crafts.
  • Whether and when to build the off-wheel cosmetic/housing system.
