Build order (slices)

Each slice is its own spec, plan, and implementation cycle.

  1. Gathering foundation (suggested first slice): universal gathering skills, proficiency to material rarity, materials as items, vendor tools, world-node spawn plus per-player harvest/respawn. Kept fully fungible, so it does not need the item-instance model. No wheel yet. Note: shipping gathering well ahead of crafting lets players stockpile raw materials and speed-level the early crafting tiers when crafting lands. The later tiers need higher-rarity inputs that only arrive with later zones and Monster Harvesting, so a zone-1 stockpile cannot carry a crafter far up the ladder; a feel concern, not a balance break. Two cheap mitigations that also fit the depth-curves-with-the-zones principle: keep zone-1 gathering to common and low-tier materials (rare and up nodes ship later with their higher zones and their crafting), and ship a handful of common recipes or a vendor sink alongside this slice so materials have an immediate use rather than piling up.
  2. Per-item-instance foundation: an instance id plus payload (signer, charges, rolled quality/stats, the boundTo recipient) across inventory, equipment, trade, market, vendor, the snapshot, the wire, and persistence. Cross-cutting, and it must land before tools, harvesting, and crafted gear, which all carry item identity. Note: only higher-tier crafts carry item identity, so common-tier crafting can ship fungibly before this slice; the instance model gates the high-tier work, not the first playable crafting.
  3. Production core: the ten crafts and their outputs, recipes, crafting, the one-active-specialty wheel (additive skill, the at-most-two active cap, adjacent-pair majors, capped opposite hobby), attunement (the per-tier quest ladder: the first-time lore quest and the repeatable make-amends switch quest), tiered mastery (the per-tier quest gate, xp lost above the current ceiling, diminishing returns below capability), combo-specific signature recipes, rarity-tier quality, ilvl-budget plus crafting-only effects, bind-to-recipient (boundTo) on high-tier gear with its commission/order workflow, and the first specialization perks. Start with low-stakes consumables, then enhancements, then gear.
  4. Crafted tools: base tool tiers (infinite durability) plus slotted effects with durability and craft-aligned recharge.
  5. Monster Harvesting plus focus: single-use corpses, component-type tags, per-corpse focus picker, the additive town focus, the time/coin/material re-spec, and signed rare+ materials with the self-gathered craft bonus.
  6. Cosmetics / market integration, wiki polish, balance.

Much later: Battlefield Experience (observed item use; committed as a concept, not needed until the core is proven); and a possible off-wheel cosmetic/housing system (Carpenter, Mason, and tending skills) if the ten feel too limited.
