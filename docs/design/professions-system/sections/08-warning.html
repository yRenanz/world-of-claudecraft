Legibility: nudges while crafting, the full picture at attunement

Legibility rests on one rule: never punish a craft, always explain a switch. Because crafting only ever adds skill, the act of crafting never costs you another craft and never needs a blocking warning. Legibility instead lives in two calmer places: gentle nudges while you craft, and the full explanation at the moment you actually change something, the attunement quest.

While crafting, the game informs, it does not warn. Common-tier crafting is silent. Crafting toward an adjacent pair surfaces an informational nudge ("you are trending toward Cogsmith; its attunement quest is available"), and nothing you craft ever lowers another skill, so there is no Cancel / Ignore dialog to dismiss. The nudges can ship first as plain info messages in the chat log, but should grow lore-friendly over time: a letter from the local Crafting Guild saying they have noticed your aptitude, or a courier who runs up to tell you the Guild Master wants a word.

At attunement, the quest spells out the whole picture before you commit: which crafts become empowered, what your title becomes, that any previously active specialty will go dormant (not lost) until you attune back, and, for a return, the escalating make-amends cost. This is the one place the system asks you to confirm a real change, and it does so in the quest, not a popup.

The one tricky edge case resolves the same calm way: two non-adjacent high crafts (Inscription to rare, then Leatherworking to rare) fail nothing and drop nothing. You simply have no adjacent pair to attune, so you remain (or attune as) a Jack of All Trades (below), with a hint on how an adjacent pair would form an archetype.
